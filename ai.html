How I work with AI.

I use AI to explore quickly, then slow down to decide carefully. It compresses the parts of design where I'd otherwise spin, but the framing, the empathy, and the final call stay mine.

✓ What I let AI do

  • Pattern-finding across messy input Surfacing themes from 30+ transcripts before I synthesize manually.
  • Drafting + pressure-testing copy First drafts, plus playing skeptical user / busy exec / new joiner.
  • Quick prototypes that test a feeling Sketch to clickable in a morning, so the team can react to something real.
  • Strategy & framework drafting Competitive teardowns, decision matrices, opportunity sizing, first pass.
  • Heuristic + accessibility checks A second pair of eyes that never gets tired before I do.

× What stays mine

  • Problem framing The instinct for what's actually worth solving comes from human conversation.
  • User empathy Real interviews, real silence, real body language. AI summarizes; it doesn't witness.
  • The final call on what ships Tradeoffs are about taste and stakes, not optimization.
  • Sensitive personal data Anything user-identifiable stays out of cloud models, full stop.
  • Original creative direction Visual taste is a muscle, outsourcing it makes it weaker.
  • Anything I'd be embarrassed to defend If I can't explain why something shipped, AI didn't help, it covered for me.
